Kivy Organization

The mission of the Kivy Organization is to promote, protect, and advance the Kivy framework, and to support and facilitate the growth of a diverse and international community of kivy programmers.

You can help fund the Kivy Organization by making a donation or becoming a member (not available yet).

Statement

The Kivy Organization is a non-profit (as per the French law of 1901) membership organization devoted to advancing open source technology related to the Kivy Framework, created in February 2014.

Role of the Organisation

Manages the funding, publicizing, protection of the core Kivy Framework, made available to the public free of charge. This includes Kivy itself, its sister projects: Python-for-android, Kivy-ios, PyJNIus, PyOBJus, Plyer, Buildozer, Kivy designer and other related projects, educational materials, and assorted tools and applications.
Establishes licenses under which the above mentioned projects are managed, ensuring the rights of the public to freely obtain, use, redistribute, and modify intellectual property held by the Kivy Organization.
Works with the Open Source Initiative to ensure that Kivy licenses conform to the Open Source Definition.
Protects the “Kivy” name, and the names, service marks and trademarks associated with all other intellectual property held by the Kivy Organization.
Raises funds to support Kivy Organization programs and services. Funding must come from private contributions (including sponsoring memberships) and public grants, must come from a broad base, and that no single private donor can supply a substantial percentage of the “Kivy Organisations” total funding. Additional revenue may be pursued in ways consistent with then-current rules for public charities and with Kivy's standing as an open source project. For example, the Kivy Organization may offer to sell conference proceedings, special KIVY distributions, or merchandise with distinctive insignia.
Publicizes, promotes the adoption of, and facilitates the ongoing development of Kivy-related technology and educational resources. This includes, but is not limited to, maintaining a public web site, planning Kivy conferences, and offering grants to Kivy-related open source projects.
Encourages and facilitates Kivy-related research in the public interest.
